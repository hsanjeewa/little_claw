Video delivery architecture

Follow a single title from the studio to your TV — encoded once, pre-positioned everywhere, streamed to fit your connection. (A simplified take on Netflix's Open Connect.)

Scroll to follow the stream ↓
01

A master arrives from the studio

The high-bitrate source is ingested and validated — one pristine copy to derive everything else from.

02

Encoded into a per-title ladder

The title is split into shots and encoded in parallel across a farm, building a quality ladder tuned by VMAF — many bitrates, multiple codecs (H.264, AV1).

03

Pre-positioned across the CDN

During off-peak "fill windows" the encoded assets are pushed out to Open Connect appliances worldwide — before anyone presses play.

04

Cached at an edge node in your ISP

An Open Connect Appliance sits inside your internet provider, so the bytes start a short hop away — not across the ocean.

05

Streamed to fit your connection

The player fetches the manifest and segments from the nearest appliance, adapting the bitrate in real time as your bandwidth shifts.
